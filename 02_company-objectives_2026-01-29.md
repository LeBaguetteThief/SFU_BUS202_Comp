# Northrop B2 Spirit - Company Objectives & Strategy

## Company Market Strategy: Customer Intimacy
Your goal is to focus on establishing a great and lasting relationship with your customers. Establishing a strong brand identity and chasing innovation will make your clients ambassadors of your business.

## Active Markets
**Note:** You will only sell your goods to the segment you have decided on here.

### Women Sneakers: Price & Quality & Customer intimacy
All three sub-segments are active in the women market.

## Targets
Aim to reach these targets through decisions denoted by semi-colons

### Financial
Financial targets aim for profitability and a high revenue for the company. The financial condition is of vital importance and is critical to running the business properly.
- **Retained profit as a percentage of Net turnover:** Goal 20%

### Customer
This is about the customer and the market. It entails market shares and the position you want to take as opposed to your direct competition within certain segments or markets.
- **Highest quality:** (2nd place out of 5 competitors) Average position measured in quality in the quality-oriented market segments.

### Internal
The internal targets are about optimizing operational processes. These processes are key in order to achieve customer oriented targets.
- **Average stock limit in days:** (30 days) The average age of inventory is the average number of days it takes for a firm to sell off inventory. Calculated as: average cost of inventory / cost of goods sold x 365.

### Learn and Growth
Regards the human capital of your company. Higher education and motivation level of your employees will increase their productivity.
- **Education level of staff:** (80%) The maximum score is 100%
- **Motivation level of staff:** (80%) The maximum score is 100%. **EXTREMELY IMPORTANT - Focus on this target the most**

## Sustainability Policy
**Important to accommodate for event shocks in the future**
- Good Health and Well-being
- Decent Work and Economic Growth
- Responsible Consumption and Production
- Reduced Inequalities

## Growth Strategy Framework
- **Product development:** You are developing new products for existing markets. How can your existing customer be better serviced and how will your products become better than those of your competitors?
